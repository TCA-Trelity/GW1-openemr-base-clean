The first five minutes

no credentials needed — dev-login is built for grading
1Open the live panel and sign in via the role picker as Physician (dev-login; nurse and resident show the role-capability gate).
2The panel opens on a patient — try William R. Thompson (wet-AMD, 7 OCTs, 4 injections). At desktop width the chat pane is already open, and its first message is the agent's opening move: what it prepared during check-in. The brief is turn zero of the conversation, not a separate report.
3Imaging tab → "Ask about this scan" → send. Watch the tool chips stream live (get_measurement_trend, compare_scans), the sparkline render in the bubble, and citations attach — click a chip to land on the source text, click a trend point to land on the exact scan.
4Ask a recommendation-shaped question — "Should I shorten his interval?" — and check the shape of the answer: record facts cited, engine/guideline output attributed in the same sentence, questions worth weighing. Never originated direction.
5Try to cross patients mid-thread — "What were Margaret Chen's medications?" — and watch the structural denial: bundle-bound tools error, foreign spans never verify, and the response gate withholds them from the wire.
