## Purpose and scope

This protocol defines how the practice uses renal function (eGFR) in the safety
screening of renally-cleared ophthalmic-relevant drugs — foremost
hydroxychloroquine (HCQ) and chloroquine (CQ). It is our operating policy: the AAO
2016 HCQ statement names renal disease as a major toxicity risk factor, and this
document translates that into concrete eGFR bands, risk tiers, and screening-interval
and coordination actions. It complements `hcq-screening`. Retinal toxicity is
bilateral, so screening applies OU.

## Why reduced eGFR raises retinal toxicity risk

HCQ and CQ are cleared substantially by the kidneys. When eGFR falls, drug clearance
drops and blood (and tissue) levels rise for the same oral dose, increasing
cumulative retinal exposure. AAO 2016 therefore lists renal disease among the major
risk factors that warrant earlier and/or more frequent screening — independent of the
mg/kg daily dose. eGFR is reported in mL/min/1.73 m².

## eGFR bands and toxicity-risk tier

The practice maps eGFR to a risk tier that modifies screening cadence. Bands follow
standard CKD staging.

| eGFR band (mL/min/1.73 m²) | CKD context | Toxicity-risk tier | Screening effect |
|----------------------------|-------------|--------------------|------------------|
| ≥ 60 | Normal / mild | Baseline (dose/duration drive risk) | Standard schedule per `hcq-screening` |
| 30–59 | Moderate (CKD stage 3) | Elevated — renal major risk factor present | Begin annual screening now (do not wait for 5 years); consider shorter interval |
| < 30 | Severe (CKD stage 4–5) | High | Screen now and shorten interval (e.g., every 6–12 months); coordinate dose review with prescriber |

A reduced eGFR alone (30–59 or < 30) counts as a major risk factor under AAO 2016,
so it moves a patient off the "no routine screening before 5 years" default even
when the daily dose is within the ≤ 5.0 mg/kg HCQ threshold.

## How reduced eGFR changes the screening interval

- **eGFR ≥ 60, no other risk factor:** follow the standard `hcq-screening` cadence
  (baseline within year one; annual after 5 years).
- **eGFR 30–59:** treat as a major-risk-factor patient — start annual screening
  immediately regardless of the 5-year mark; document the elevated tier.
- **eGFR < 30:** start screening immediately and shorten the interval below annual
  where clinically indicated; escalate coordination with the prescriber about dose
  reduction, since clearance is markedly reduced.
- Any **acute decline in eGFR** between visits is a prompt to re-tier and re-examine
  sooner, not to wait for the next scheduled screen.

## Documentation of outside-lab review

Renal values usually arrive from outside labs. The practice policy:

- Record the eGFR value, the collection date, and the source; mark it **unverified
  (outside record)** until confirmed against the source document.
- Never silently overwrite a prior renal value — supersede it with the newer dated
  result and keep the lineage (consistent with `intake-documentation-standards`).
- If two sources disagree, surface the discrepancy rather than merging; the
  physician resolves which value drives the risk tier.
- The eGFR that sets the current risk tier must be the one shown next to the screening
  decision, with its date, so staleness is visible.

## Coordination with the prescriber

When reduced eGFR raises the tier, generate a coordination note to the prescribing
clinician (rheumatology, PCP, or dermatology) stating the eGFR, its date, the
resulting toxicity-risk tier, and the ophthalmic action taken (earlier/shorter
screening). The practice does not adjust systemic dosing — it surfaces the renal
signal and its guideline attribution so the prescriber can weigh a dose change. This
preserves the not-a-CDS posture: facts and references, not directives.

## Action triggers

- New eGFR crossing below 60 → re-tier to elevated, begin/continue annual screening.
- New eGFR below 30, or a rapid decline → re-tier to high, screen now, shorten
  interval, escalate prescriber coordination.
- Concurrent tamoxifen plus reduced eGFR → compounded risk; treat as high tier.

## Documentation requirements

Record: the eGFR value/date/source and its verification status, the assigned
toxicity-risk tier and the band that set it, the resulting screening interval and
its change from prior, any coordination note sent, and cross-reference to the
`hcq-screening` encounter. Every value carries its source.
