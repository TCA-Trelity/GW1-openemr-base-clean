## Purpose and scope

This protocol standardizes how the practice stages diabetic retinopathy (DR), sets
follow-up cadence, classifies diabetic macular edema (DME), and selects first-line
treatment. It is our operating standard, grounded in the AAO Preferred Practice
Pattern (PPP) for Diabetic Retinopathy and DRCR Retina Network evidence. Findings
are staged per eye (independently OD and OS).

## Severity staging (International/ETDRS-derived scale)

The severity ladder is the International Clinical DR Severity Scale, derived from
the ETDRS; severe NPDR is defined by the ETDRS **4-2-1 rule**.

| Stage | Defining findings |
|-------|-------------------|
| No apparent retinopathy | No microaneurysms or other lesions |
| Mild NPDR | Microaneurysms only |
| Moderate NPDR | More than microaneurysms, but less than severe NPDR |
| Severe NPDR (4-2-1 rule) | Any of: > 20 intraretinal hemorrhages in EACH of 4 quadrants; definite venous beading in ≥ 2 quadrants; prominent IRMA in ≥ 1 quadrant — and no signs of PDR |
| PDR | Neovascularization (NVD/NVE) and/or vitreous or preretinal hemorrhage |

High-risk PDR (DRS criteria) = NVD ≥ 1/4–1/3 disc area; OR any NVD with vitreous/preretinal hemorrhage; OR NVE ≥ 1/2 disc area with vitreous/preretinal hemorrhage. High-risk PDR is a treatment trigger, not an observation state.

## Follow-up intervals by severity (no DME present)

Per AAO PPP, cadence tightens as severity rises. Intervals below assume no DME; any
DME shortens follow-up independently (see the DME sections).

| Severity (no DME) | Follow-up interval |
|-------------------|--------------------|
| No apparent DR | 12 months |
| Mild NPDR | 6–12 months |
| Moderate NPDR | 3–6 months |
| Severe NPDR | 3–4 months |
| Non-high-risk PDR | 2–4 months |
| High-risk PDR | Treat; follow per treatment response |

## DME classification (center-involved is the treatment pivot)

Two definitions coexist; the practice records both the OCT-based classification and,
where relevant, the older biomicroscopic CSME criteria.

- **Center-involved DME (CI-DME)** — thickening involving the central 1-mm subfield
  on OCT. This is the modern treatment pivot.
- **Non-center-involved DME (non-CI-DME)** — thickening sparing the central 1-mm subfield.
- **Clinically significant macular edema (CSME, ETDRS)** — any of: thickening within
  500 µm of the foveal center; hard exudates within 500 µm of center with adjacent
  thickening; a zone of thickening ≥ 1 disc area, any part within 1 disc diameter of center.

## First-line treatment for center-involved DME

Per AAO PPP and DRCR Retina Network evidence:

- **CI-DME with vision impairment: anti-VEGF is first-line** (aflibercept,
  ranibizumab, or bevacizumab; DRCR Protocol T). Begin promptly.
- **CI-DME with good vision (≈ 20/25 or better):** observe with prompt anti-VEGF if
  acuity declines (DRCR Protocol V).
- **Non-CI-DME without central threat:** observe or treat focally. Focal/grid laser
  is an adjunct for persistent non-central edema, not first-line for central disease.

## Treatment of proliferative DR (anti-VEGF vs PRP)

- **Panretinal photocoagulation (PRP)** remains a PDR standard, preferred when
  reliable follow-up is uncertain since its effect persists without monthly visits.
- **Anti-VEGF** is a validated alternative: DRCR Protocol S found ranibizumab
  non-inferior to PRP at 2 and 5 years, and it is favored with concurrent CI-DME
  (choice weighs adherence, coexisting DME, field preservation, and cost).

## Referral and action triggers

Flag for physician review / retina management when: severe NPDR or worse; any PDR
(urgent for high-risk PDR); new CI-DME with vision loss; vitreous hemorrhage; or
signs of tractional detachment or neovascular glaucoma. Rapid progression between
visits shortens the next interval.

## Documentation requirements

Record per eye: DR severity stage with the findings that set it, DME status (CI vs non-CI, central subfield thickness if measured), treatments with dates, and the assigned follow-up interval with rationale. Every finding carries laterality and source.
