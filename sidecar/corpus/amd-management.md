## Purpose and scope

This protocol standardizes how the practice classifies age-related macular
degeneration (AMD), determines AREDS2 supplement eligibility, sets home monitoring,
and initiates and monitors anti-VEGF therapy for neovascular AMD (nAMD). It is our
operating standard, grounded in the AAO Preferred Practice Pattern for AMD and the
AREDS/AREDS2 trial evidence. AMD is staged per eye; both eyes are assessed (OU).

## Classification by drusen size and pigmentary change

The practice uses the Beckman clinical classification (drusen sized at the widest
point; pigmentary change = any definite AMD-associated hyper/hypopigmentation).

| Category | Defining findings |
|----------|-------------------|
| No apparent aging changes | No drusen, no pigmentary changes |
| Normal aging changes | Small drusen (drupelets) ≤ 63 µm only |
| Early AMD | Medium drusen > 63 µm and ≤ 125 µm; no pigmentary changes |
| Intermediate AMD | Large drusen > 125 µm and/or any AMD pigmentary changes |
| Advanced AMD | Advanced dry (geographic atrophy involving/threatening fovea) and/or neovascular/exudative AMD (choroidal neovascularization, CNV) |

## AREDS2 supplement criteria (who benefits, and the formula)

Per AREDS/AREDS2, supplementation reduces the ~5-year risk of progression to
advanced AMD by roughly 25% in eligible patients. Eligibility, per AAO PPP:

- **Recommend AREDS2 supplements** for: intermediate AMD (large drusen in one or
  both eyes) OR advanced AMD (GA or nAMD) in one eye.
- **Do not recommend** for early AMD or no AMD — no proven benefit.

| AREDS2 daily formula | Amount |
|----------------------|--------|
| Vitamin C | 500 mg |
| Vitamin E | 400 IU |
| Lutein | 10 mg |
| Zeaxanthin | 2 mg |
| Zinc (as zinc oxide) | 80 mg |
| Copper (as cupric oxide) | 2 mg |

AREDS2 removed beta-carotene (it raised lung-cancer risk in current and former
smokers) and replaced it with lutein/zeaxanthin; beta-carotene-containing formulas
must be avoided in anyone with a smoking history.

## Home monitoring for progression to neovascular AMD

- **Amsler grid** daily (one eye at a time), reporting new distortion or scotoma.
- For **intermediate AMD**, home preferential-hyperacuity-perimetry tele-monitoring
  (AREDS2-HOME evidence) can detect nAMD conversion before patient-noticed symptoms.
- Any sudden distortion, new central blur, or acute vision drop is a same-week trigger.

## Anti-VEGF initiation for neovascular AMD

- **Confirm active nAMD** on OCT (intraretinal/subretinal/sub-RPE fluid or a CNV
  lesion), with exam and, where needed, OCT-angiography or fluorescein angiography.
- **Initiate anti-VEGF promptly** on confirmed active exudative disease (aflibercept,
  ranibizumab, bevacizumab, brolucizumab, or faricimab) — earlier treatment preserves vision.
- Maintenance (loading then interval extension) follows `anti-vegf-treat-and-extend`.

## Monitoring intervals by category

| Category | Monitoring cadence |
|----------|--------------------|
| Early AMD | Exam every 6–24 months; home Amsler between visits |
| Intermediate AMD | Exam every 6–12 months; home monitoring; OCT as indicated |
| Advanced dry AMD (GA) | Every 6–12 months; monitor fellow eye for CNV |
| Active nAMD under treatment | Per treat-and-extend interval (see cross-referenced protocol) |

## Referral and action triggers

Flag for physician review / urgent retina evaluation when: new metamorphopsia or
acute central vision loss; new OCT fluid or hemorrhage in a previously dry eye;
suspected CNV; or fellow-eye conversion in a patient already treated in one eye.
The fellow eye of a unilateral nAMD patient is high-risk and is watched closely.

## Documentation requirements

Record per eye: AMD category with the drusen/pigment findings that set it, AREDS2
eligibility and current supplement use (and smoking status for beta-carotene
safety), home-monitoring method, nAMD activity on OCT, treatments with dates, and
the monitoring interval assigned. Every finding carries laterality and source.
