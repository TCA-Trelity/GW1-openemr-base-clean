## Purpose and scope

Diabetic retinopathy progression is modifiable by systemic control. This protocol
records the glycemic, blood-pressure, lipid, and renal targets the practice tracks
as retinopathy risk modifiers, and defines when out-of-range labs change ophthalmic
management or trigger coordination with the primary care physician (PCP) or
endocrinology. Targets are cited from the ADA Standards of Care as a coordination
reference — the practice does not manage systemic disease, it aligns eye-care
cadence to it. Applies to both eyes.

## Glycemic target and its retinal effect (HbA1c)

Per ADA Standards of Care, the general HbA1c goal for many nonpregnant adults is
**< 7%**, individualized (a less stringent goal such as < 8% may be appropriate for
limited life expectancy, extensive comorbidity, or hypoglycemia risk). DCCT and
UKPDS established that tighter glycemic control reduces the incidence and
progression of retinopathy.

| Parameter | Target (ADA, individualized) | Retinal relevance |
|-----------|------------------------------|-------------------|
| HbA1c | < 7% for many adults (up to < 8% when individualized) | Higher and rising HbA1c predicts faster DR progression |

**Early-worsening caution:** rapid intensification of glycemic control can cause
transient early worsening of retinopathy. When a patient reports a large recent
HbA1c drop, examine sooner rather than deferring.

## Blood-pressure target and its retinal effect

Per ADA Standards of Care, a blood-pressure goal of **< 130/80 mmHg** is
recommended for many adults with diabetes and hypertension when it can be reached
safely. UKPDS showed that tighter blood-pressure control slows retinopathy
progression.

| Parameter | Target (ADA) | Retinal relevance |
|-----------|--------------|-------------------|
| Blood pressure | < 130/80 mmHg (individualized) | Hypertension accelerates DR and DME |

## Lipid management and its retinal effect

Statin therapy is guided by ASCVD risk (ADA). Beyond lipid lowering, fenofibrate
slowed DR progression in the FIELD and ACCORD-Eye trials, independent of lipid
levels. The practice records lipid status and fibrate use as DR risk modifiers and
defers pharmacologic management to the PCP.

## Renal function as a DR and drug-safety signal

Diabetic nephropathy correlates with retinopathy severity; declining eGFR or new
albuminuria is a marker of microvascular progression and a prompt to review
retinopathy cadence. Declining eGFR also independently affects renally-cleared
ocular-relevant drugs (see `renal-function-ocular-drug-safety`).

## When out-of-range systemic values change ophthalmic management

The practice tightens eye-care cadence and coordinates outward when systemic
control deteriorates:

- **Rising or persistently high HbA1c** (e.g., well above the individualized target)
  in a patient with existing DR → shorten the next retinal follow-up and notify the PCP.
- **Uncontrolled blood pressure** with DME or worsening DR → notify the PCP/managing
  clinician; document that eye findings may not stabilize until BP is controlled.
- **Declining eGFR / new significant albuminuria** → treat as a progression signal;
  re-examine sooner and coordinate.
- **Pregnancy or planned pregnancy** → examine each trimester (rapid progression risk).

## PCP / endocrinology coordination protocol

When a reviewed outside lab is out of range and clinically relevant to retinopathy:
(1) document the value, its date, and its source as an unverified outside result
until confirmed; (2) generate a coordination note to the PCP/endocrinology naming
the specific value and its ophthalmic implication; (3) never restate a systemic
target as a prescription — the practice surfaces the number and its guideline
attribution, the managing clinician sets therapy. This preserves the not-a-CDS
posture: facts and guideline references, not directives.

## Documentation requirements

Record the most recent HbA1c, blood pressure, lipid panel, and eGFR with their
dates and sources; whether each is in or out of the ADA-referenced range; any
coordination note sent; and any change to retinal follow-up interval with rationale.
