## Purpose and scope

This protocol defines how the practice screens patients on hydroxychloroquine
(HCQ) or chloroquine (CQ) for drug-induced retinopathy. It is our in-house
standard, grounded in the American Academy of Ophthalmology's 2016 revised
screening recommendations (Marmor et al.). Toxicity is irreversible and can
progress after the drug stops, so the goal is detection at a subclinical stage,
before visible bull's-eye maculopathy (already advanced disease). Applies OU.

## Dosing risk threshold (daily dose by real body weight)

Risk is driven primarily by daily dose per actual (real) body weight and cumulative duration; the 2016 AAO revision replaced ideal- with real-body-weight dosing.

| Drug | Daily dose threshold (AAO 2016) | Basis |
|------|----------------------------------|-------|
| Hydroxychloroquine | > 5.0 mg/kg real body weight | Major risk factor above this |
| Chloroquine | > 2.3 mg/kg real body weight | Major risk factor above this |

Per AAO 2016, at or below 5.0 mg/kg HCQ real weight with no other risk factors,
estimated retinopathy prevalence is under 1% in the first 5 years and under 2% up
to 10 years, rising to nearly 20% after 20 years of use. Record the computed
mg/kg/day at each visit — the model surfaces it, the physician confirms it.

## Major risk factors (any one raises screening priority)

Per AAO 2016, each factor independently elevates toxicity risk; any one triggers
screening before the routine 5-year mark and consideration of a shorter interval:

- **Daily dose** above thresholds (HCQ > 5.0 mg/kg, CQ > 2.3 mg/kg real weight).
- **Duration of use** > 5 years (at acceptable dose, no other risk factors).
- **Renal disease / reduced eGFR** — renally cleared, so impaired clearance raises
  blood levels (see `renal-function-ocular-drug-safety`).
- **Concurrent tamoxifen use** — AAO 2016 cites an ~5-fold increase in risk.
- **Pre-existing retinal or macular disease** — confounds interpretation, raises baseline risk.

## Screening schedule (baseline plus annual after five years)

| Timepoint | Action | Condition |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Within first year of starting drug | Baseline exam | All patients; rule out pre-existing maculopathy |
| Years 1–5 | No routine annual screening | Only if acceptable dose AND no major risk factors |
| After 5 years of use | Begin annual screening | Standard-risk patients |
| Any time major risk factor present | Begin annual screening immediately | Do not wait for the 5-year mark |

The baseline exam sets a reference and excludes confounding pre-existing
maculopathy. Annual is the default once started; higher-risk patients may be screened more often.

## Screening modalities (primary tests and adjuncts)

Per AAO 2016, two primary objective tests are used together, with two adjuncts:

- **SD-OCT** (spectral-domain OCT) — structural; parafoveal ellipsoid-zone/outer-retinal thinning.
- **Automated visual field 10-2** — functional; parafoveal sensitivity loss.
- **mfERG** (multifocal ERG) *(adjunct)* — objective corroboration; can substitute for VF.
- **FAF** (fundus autofluorescence) *(adjunct)* — highlights RPE damage topographically.

AAO 2016 does **not** recommend as primary screening: Amsler grid, color vision,
full-field ERG, EOG, fluorescein angiography, time-domain OCT, or fundus photography.

## Asian-ancestry pattern note (visual-field field size)

Toxicity in patients of Asian descent frequently presents in an **extramacular /
pericentral** distribution, outside the central macula a 10-2 field samples. For
these patients, AAO 2016 advises a wider field — **24-2 or 30-2** — in addition to
(not instead of) 10-2 and SD-OCT, and interpreting OCT beyond the parafovea.
Missing the pericentral pattern is the classic screening error here.

## Cessation and referral triggers

- When SD-OCT, 10-2 VF, mfERG, or FAF show definite signs of toxicity, flag for
  physician review and **coordinate discontinuation with the prescriber** — stopping
  weighs systemic disease control against ocular risk.
- Counsel that toxicity may progress for months to years after cessation; document it.
- Confirmed or strongly suspected retinopathy warrants retina-specialist confirmation.

## Documentation requirements

Record at each encounter: drug, daily dose, computed mg/kg/day on real body weight,
cumulative duration and estimated cumulative dose, renal status (most recent eGFR
and date), tamoxifen use, tests performed with results, and the assigned screening
interval with its rationale. Every value carries its source.
